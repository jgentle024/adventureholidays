The Alps Await

A climbing holiday in the French Alps is a fantastic way to experience the breathtaking landscapes while challenging yourself on some of the most iconic peaks in the region. Below is a 7-day itinerary that mixes rock climbing, mountaineering, and rest days, with a focus on iconic climbing spots in the French Alps.

Day 1: Arrival in Chamonix Arrive in Chamonix, the epicenter of alpine climbing, easily accessible from Geneva by train or car. Spend the day acclimatizing and exploring the town. Visit Aiguille du Midi for incredible views of Mont Blanc and the surrounding mountains. This is a great place to get a sense of the scale and beauty of the region. Use this day to rent climbing gear and get advice from the local climbing shops, which can also offer guided climbing excursions if needed.

Day 2: Classic Rock Climbing at Aiguille du Midi Morning: Take the Aiguille du Midi cable car up to 3,842 meters. From here, you’ll be at the heart of some of the most famous climbing spots in the world. Climbing Option 1: Cosmiques Arete (AD) – A classic alpine rock climb that starts from the Cosmiques Hut and offers spectacular views of Mont Blanc. The climb is about 4-6 hours and is a great intro to high-altitude alpine climbing. Climbing Option 2: If you're looking for a more technical challenge, you could attempt The Gouter Route on Mont Blanc (if you're experienced and well-acclimatized). Evening: Head back to Chamonix for a well-deserved rest. Enjoy the town’s vibrant climbing community and dine at one of the many local restaurants offering hearty alpine fare.

Day 3: Mountaineering on Mont Blanc (Gouter Route) Early Morning: Meet with a guide (if necessary) for a Gouter Route ascent on Mont Blanc (4,810 meters). This is one of the most popular routes to the summit, involving technical climbing and glacier travel. The climb generally takes 8-10 hours and involves sections of rock climbing and glacier crossing, including a night at the Gouter Hut (3,835 meters). Alternative: If you are not ready for the full Mont Blanc ascent, you can choose other mountaineering peaks like Aiguille de Bionassay or Dôme du Goûter, both offering breathtaking views and technical climbing. Evening: Stay at the Gouter Hut or descend to Chamonix.

Day 4: Rock Climbing in the Verdon Gorge Morning: Take a scenic drive to the Verdon Gorge in southern France, a world-renowned climbing destination. It’s a bit of a journey (around 4 hours from Chamonix), but well worth it for a day of stunning rock climbing. Climbing: The Verdon Gorge is famous for its exposed limestone cliffs that rise up from the turquoise river below. Choose from multi-pitch sport climbing routes or more traditional rock climbing routes, such as Le Cantaret or La Directe des Crêtes. Climbing Level: The area offers climbing for all levels, with classic routes ranging from moderate to highly challenging (grades 4-8a). Evening: Spend the night in Moustiers-Sainte-Marie, a charming village near the gorge, or camp out if you prefer.

Day 5: Climbing in the Ecrins National Park Morning: Travel to Écrins National Park, located near the town of Le Bourg-d’Oisans (about 3.5 hours from Verdon). This region offers some of the most breathtaking and technical alpine climbing in the Alps. Climbing: Try the Ailefroide Glacier or La Meije (which offers a challenging summit), or opt for classic routes like the Pic Sans Nom or Dôme des Glaciers. You can also try an easier, but equally beautiful route like Les Bans or Les Agneaux. Evening: Stay in La Grave (a climber’s hub) for the night. This is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding climbing areas.

Day 6: Alpine Rock Climbing in the Aiguilles Rouges Morning: Head back to the Chamonix region, and spend the day in the Aiguilles Rouges massif, which offers less technical but very scenic alpine rock climbing routes. Climbing: Opt for classic routes like Aiguille de la Flégère or the Aiguille de l’M. These routes provide stunning views of Mont Blanc and the surrounding peaks. Climbing Level: These climbs tend to be of moderate difficulty, but the exposure and views are fantastic. Evening: Return to Chamonix for your last night in town. Enjoy the vibrant après-climb scene in one of the alpine lodges.

Day 7: Rest Day or Optional Climbing Rest Day: If you're feeling exhausted, take a rest day in Chamonix. You can explore the town, relax at a spa, or enjoy a light hike in the area (such as the Grand Balcon Nord). Optional Climbing: If you're still up for some action, consider a half-day climb at La Jonction or an easy sport climbing session at Les Gaillands crag just outside Chamonix. Evening: Head back to your base, pack, and prepare for departure.

This trip will cost approximately €1,500-€2,500 per person, depending on accommodation choices, climbing guides, and additional activities. The itinerary can be adjusted based on your climbing experience and preferences. Tips for Your Climbing Holiday in the French Alps: 1.Guides and Equipment: If you're new to alpine climbing or mountaineering, it's highly recommended to hire a local guide for technical climbs (especially in high-altitude areas). You can rent gear in Chamonix or other climbing towns. 2.Acclimatization: The high-altitude climbs can be demanding. Make sure you spend a couple of days acclimatizing and drinking plenty of water. Never underestimate the effects of altitude. 3.Fitness: Some of the climbs require advanced technical skills and a good level of fitness. If you're not a seasoned climber, focus on improving your endurance and technique before the trip. 4.Weather Conditions: The weather in the Alps can change quickly, so always check the forecast and be prepared for sudden storms. Carry appropriate clothing, including layers, rain gear, and sunscreen. 5.Respect Local Regulations: Always follow local environmental and safety regulations, especially in national parks and protected areas.
