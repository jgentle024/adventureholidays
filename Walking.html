The Alps Await

A hiking holiday in the French Alps is an amazing way to explore stunning landscapes, charming villages, and breathtaking mountain peaks. Here’s a sample itinerary for a 7-day hiking holiday in the French Alps that offers a balance of challenging hikes, scenic views, and some time to relax and enjoy the local culture.

Day 1: Arrival in Chamonix - Arrive in Chamonix (easily accessible via train from Geneva or by car). Spend the day exploring the charming alpine town, which is a hub for mountaineers and outdoor enthusiasts. Visit the Aiguille du Midi for stunning panoramic views of Mont Blanc. Get an early night to rest up for the hikes ahead.

Day 2: Hike to Lac Blanc - Start the day with a cable car ride from Chamonix to the Index lift, followed by a moderate hike to Lac Blanc. The hike to Lac Blanc is about 3-4 hours one-way, with stunning views of Mont Blanc and the surrounding peaks. Spend time at the lake, which is surrounded by incredible alpine scenery, and enjoy a picnic by the water. Descend back to Chamonix in the afternoon and relax.

Day 3: Col de Balme and Swiss Border - Take a bus or taxi to Le Tour, a small village near Chamonix. Hike up to Col de Balme (2,190 meters) for impressive views of the French-Swiss border and the Chamonix valley. The hike to the summit is about 5-6 hours, and it’s a perfect opportunity to enjoy the high-altitude alpine meadows. If you have time and energy, consider continuing to Trient, a village on the Swiss side of the border. Return to Chamonix in the evening.

Day 4: Glacier du Mont Glacier - Spend the day at Mer de Glace (Sea of Ice), the largest glacier in France. Take the Montenvers Railway up to Mer de Glace and explore the glacier, the ice cave, and the Glacier Museum. For a hiking adventure, take the Glacier des Bossons trail or the Grand Balcon Nord trail, which provides spectacular views of Mont Blanc and the surrounding mountains. Head back to Chamonix in the evening and enjoy a cozy dinner in the town.

Day 5: Hiking in Vanoise National Park - Travel to the Vanoise National Park, located between the Tarentaise and Maurienne valleys (around a 2-hour drive from Chamonix). Hike the Pralognan-la-Vanoise to Refuge de la Vanoise trail. This is a stunning hike through alpine meadows, with panoramic views of glaciers and rugged peaks. The hike will take about 4-5 hours and ends at the refuge, where you can enjoy a warm meal and sleep in a traditional alpine hut. Alternatively, if you prefer, you can stay in a nearby village like Bozel and explore the park's trails the next day.

Day 6: Hike to Col de la Vanoise - From the Refuge de la Vanoise, head out on a longer, more challenging hike to the Col de la Vanoise (2,520 meters). The hike is about 5-6 hours and offers stunning views of glaciers, mountain lakes, and wildlife like marmots and ibex. After reaching the col, return to your starting point or choose to continue exploring the area before returning to the village for the night.

Day 7: Return to Chamonix - Head back to Chamonix or your departure location. If time allows, take a final short hike or explore the Chamonix Valley, visit the Mont Blanc massif or enjoy the Mer de Glace one last time before heading home.

Tips: 1.Accommodation: Plan for a mix of hotels, mountain lodges, and refuges to experience the alpine charm of the region. Many of the mountain refuges require advanced booking. 2.Packing: Be sure to pack light but also bring the essentials—layers, good hiking boots, rain gear, sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water. 3.Fitness: Some of the hikes are moderately challenging, so make sure you’re comfortable with mountain hikes that have elevation gain and rocky terrain. 4.Mountain Safety: Be aware of the weather conditions in the Alps as they can change quickly. Always check forecasts, and carry a map or GPS device. 5.Transport: There are excellent public transport connections (bus, trains, cable cars) in the region, but renting a car will provide more flexibility to explore at your own pace.
